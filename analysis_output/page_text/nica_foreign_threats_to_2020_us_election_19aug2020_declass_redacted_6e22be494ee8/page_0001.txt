DECLASSIFIED BY PRESIDENT TRUMP on 3 July 2026 NATIONAL INTELLIGENCE COUNCIL ASSESSMENT 1111111111 I 9 August 2020 NICA 2020-06885D - Foreign Threats to 2020 US Federal Elections (U) Key Takeaways - Scope Note: This is a downgraded version of a paper originally published on 19 August, 2020, and includes information current as of that date. This paper updates NICM 2020-32, and NICM 2020-033, which also addresses China's broader influence efforts. This paper does not make assessments about the impact of these efforts on the US. It also does not discuss efforts that the IC cannot attribute to foreign actors, (U) Ahead of the 2020 US elections, foreign states will continue to use covert and overt influence measures in their attempts to sway US voters' preferences and perspectives, shift US policies, increase discord in the United States, and undermine the American people's confidence in our democratic processes. • - Foreign states or other actors may seek to compromise our election infrastrncture for a range of possible purposes, such as interfering with the voting process, stealing sensitive data, or calling into question the validity of the election results. We judge that Russia, China, Iran, as well as many nonstate actors, have the capability to conduct such activities, although it would be difficult for them to manipulate voting processes at scale and without detection. We are primarily concerned about ongoing and potential influence activity by Russia, China, and Iran. • We assess that Russia is using a range of measures primarily to denigrate former Vice President Eiden and what it sees as an anti-Russia establishment. For example, it is directing or encouraging proxies to spread claims about Vice President Eiden. Some Kremlin- linked actors are also seeking to boost President Trnmp's candidacy on social media. More broadly, Moscow's efforts seek to amplify social discord in the US and undermine Washington's global standing. • We assess that China prefers that President Trnmp be defeated; in the past few months it has stepped up public rhetoric criticizing his Administration, which it recognizes may affect the election.
