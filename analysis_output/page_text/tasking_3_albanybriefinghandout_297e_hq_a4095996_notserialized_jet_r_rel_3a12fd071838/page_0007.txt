(For more information, see the Bureau Control System BLUEGEM Policy Guide {1012PG] [links to a SECRET//NOFORN policy document)). 9.2. (U) Thresholds for Raw Intelligence Product Types (U//FOUO) All raw intelligence product types must meet the “DRAIN” threshold to be suitable for release as FBI raw intelligence reports. Additional threshold requirements exist for each type of raw reporting as well. 9.2.1. (U) DRAIN Threshold (U//FOUO) All four raw intelligence product types must meet the DRAIN threshold. DRAIN stands for: 1. (U) Detailed 2. (U) Respectful of the rights of USPERs 3. (U) Authoritative 4. (U) Of Interest 5. (U) New (U) For additional guidance on meeting the DRAIN threshold, see the FBI Raw Jntelligence Reports Manual. 9.2.1.1. (U//FOUO) Detailed (U//FOUO) All raw intelligence reports must contain actionable intelligence and must be ~ sufficiently detailed to be of operational, investigative, or analytical value to recipients. 9.2.1.2. (U//FOUO) Respectful of the Rights of United States Persons (U//FOUO) All raw intelligence reports must be respectful of the rights of USPERs to participate in constitutionally protected activities, such as freedom of speech and the right to assemble. A raw report cannot solely describe activities and the expression of thoughts that are protected by the U.S. Constitution. Raw reports may include USPER information only if (1) the USPER’s identity is necessary to understand the report or to assess its importance, (2) dissemination is limited to entities that have the subject matter of the report as part of their authorized functions, and (3) the identification of the USPER does not compromise any operation or investigation. The IPM (or designee) must determine that these requirements have been met in order to identify an USPER. The authoring FBIHQ division or FO does not need to obtain legal review to name the USPER unless the raw report meets another legal review trigger, as articulated in subsections 9.2.2.3. and 9.4.4. of this PG. 40 UNCLASSIFIED//FOUO
